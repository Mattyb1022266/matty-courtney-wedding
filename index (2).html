first-timer's guide
Disney Tips
Making the most of every day
✦
before you arrive
📱
Download My Disney Experience
Your essential Disney app. Check live ride wait times, mobile order food, book Lightning Lane passes & navigate all four parks. Download before you fly and set it up.
⌚
Get a MagicBand or use your phone
Your MagicBand is your room key, park ticket and payment card on your wrist. You can buy one at the hotel or just use the Disney app on your phone — both work perfectly.
🎢
Book Lightning Lane passes early
Lightning Lane is Disney's skip-the-queue system. As resort guests you can book up to 7 days before each park visit. The most popular rides go fast — book first thing each morning.
in the parks
🌅
Use your early entry every single day
30 minutes before the public opens the gates is worth more than any paid pass. Head straight for the busiest rides — queues are a fraction of what they'll be by 10am.
🌦️
Expect afternoon rain — don't panic
April in Florida means short, sharp afternoon thunderstorms. Pack a small poncho (not an umbrella — too awkward). The rain usually clears within 30 minutes and the air cools beautifully.
💧
Free iced water everywhere
Any quick-service restaurant in the parks will give you a free cup of iced water if you ask. Bring a refillable bottle — there are water fill stations throughout all four parks.
🍔
Mobile order your food
Use My Disney Experience to pre-order from counter service restaurants. You skip the queue entirely and just collect when it's ready. Saves loads of time at busy lunch periods.
👟
Wear the most comfortable shoes you own
You will walk 15,000–25,000 steps a day at Disney. Blisters will ruin your trip faster than anything else. No new shoes — wear your most broken-in, comfortable trainers.
saving money
💳
Use a fee-free travel card
Monzo, Starling and Chase are all brilliant for the US — no foreign transaction fees and great exchange rates. Set one up before you fly and use it for everything.
🎁
Buy souvenirs on your last day
With 14 days there's no rush! Disney lets you ship purchases to the resort hotel so you don't carry anything around the parks. Don't blow the budget on day one.
🧃
Get a Rapid Fill mug at the hotel
One mug (~$23) gets you unlimited soft drinks and hot drinks from the resort food court for your entire 14-night stay. Easily the best value purchase of the whole trip.
🎪
Take a rest day — you'll need it
With 14 days you don't need a park every day. The resort pool, Disney Springs (free entry, great food and shopping) or just a lie-in are all valid options. The parks are exhausting!
uk-specific tips
✈️
Getting from the airport to the resort
Disney's old free Magical Express no longer runs. From Orlando Airport (MCO), take a Mears Connect shuttle or Uber/Lyft — about $35–45 and a 30-minute drive. Book Mears in advance at mearstransportation.com.
🕐
Jet lag is real — plan for it
Orlando is 5 hours behind the UK. You'll likely wake at 5am for the first few days — actually brilliant for early park entry! Don't plan anything too late on your first evening.
🌡️
April weather in Florida
Expect 27–30°C (80–86°F) with sunshine and humidity. Pack light breathable clothing, good walking shoes, SPF50+ sunscreen and sunglasses. Evenings can be slightly cooler so bring a light layer.
🔌
US plugs & power
The US uses Type A/B plugs at 120V. Bring a universal travel adaptor. Most modern phone chargers are dual voltage (check for 100–240V on the plug) so they'll work fine with just an adaptor.
getting around
Transport
Disney World is brilliantly connected
✦
As resort guests at All Star Movies, all Disney transport is completely free. You genuinely don't need a hire car — Disney's system covers everywhere you'll want to go.
🚌
Disney Buses
Main transport from All Star Movies
Direct buses run from All Star Movies to all 4 parks, both water parks and Disney Springs. A tip: All Star Movies is usually the last stop on the route so allow extra time. Aim to catch the bus 60 minutes before your target park opens to make the most of early entry.
🚡
Disney Skyliner
Gondola gondola system — worth experiencing
The Skyliner gondola connects several resorts to EPCOT and Hollywood Studios. You can't board it directly from All Star Movies, but hop on a bus to Pop Century Resort and jump on from there. The views from 60ft up are spectacular — absolutely worth doing at least once.
🚝
Monorail
Magic Kingdom & EPCOT loop
The monorail loops between Magic Kingdom, the Grand Floridian, Polynesian, Contemporary resorts and EPCOT. Take a bus to Magic Kingdom, then ride the monorail onward — it's a classic Disney experience and the views of Cinderella Castle are stunning.
🚤
Ferry Boats
Magic Kingdom approach
A large ferry boat crosses the Seven Seas Lagoon to Magic Kingdom from the Transportation & Ticket Center. It's slower than the monorail but far more scenic — a proper Disney arrival moment. Highly recommended for your first Magic Kingdom visit.
🚗
Uber & Lyft
For quick trips or days out
Both apps work perfectly at Disney World. Great if you want a day at Universal Studios (about 20 mins away) or if bus waits get long. Minnie Vans are Disney-themed Lyfts seating up to 6 — fun but 2–3x more expensive than a regular Lyft. Regular rideshare costs around £10–20 within the resort area.
💡
Check the app before heading out
My Disney Experience shows live bus wait times. Check it before leaving your room — if a bus is 5 mins away, you can time your walk perfectly.
